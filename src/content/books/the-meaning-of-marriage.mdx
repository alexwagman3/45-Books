## Summary

Tim Keller argues that marriage is not primarily about personal fulfillment but about mutual sacrifice that paradoxically leads to the deepest joy. Drawing on Ephesians 5, Keller shows that marriage is designed to reflect the gospel — the costly, self-giving love of Christ for the church.

The book covers friendship as the foundation of marriage, the power of grace for forgiveness, embracing gender differences, love languages, and the mission of helping each other grow into who God created you to be. Read this before marriage and during it. Implement it by choosing one chapter per month to read with your spouse, then identifying one specific way to love them more sacrificially this week. This book will change how you see your spouse — as someone God gave you to help sanctify, not satisfy.
