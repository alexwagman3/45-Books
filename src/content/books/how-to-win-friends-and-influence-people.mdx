## Summary

Carnegie's classic distills decades of research into the art of human relations — the practical mechanics of making people feel valued, resolving conflict without creating resentment, and becoming the kind of person others genuinely want to be around. The principles are deceptively simple: become genuinely interested in others, remember their name, listen more than you talk, and make people feel important. But doing these things consistently is where the real work lives. What makes this book endure is that it treats social skills as a discipline — something you practice and refine, not something you're born with. Read this once to get the principles, then read it again with a specific relationship in mind. The difference between people who read it and people who live it is practice. Implement one principle per week, in one specific relationship, until it becomes instinct.
