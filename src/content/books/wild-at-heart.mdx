## Summary

Wild at Heart argues that God created men with three core desires: a battle to fight, an adventure to live, and a beauty to rescue — and that modern life systematically suppresses all three. Eldredge writes with pastoral honesty about the wounds that leave men passive, performance-driven, or disconnected from their own hearts, and points toward a life of genuine risk, purpose, and love. This is not primarily a book about masculinity theory — it is an invitation to recover the wildness, courage, and adventure that God wired into men from the beginning. Read this if you feel tame, bored, or like you're performing a role rather than living a life. Implement it by identifying your wound, naming the false story you've believed about yourself, and taking one genuine risk per month — in relationship, work, or adventure.
