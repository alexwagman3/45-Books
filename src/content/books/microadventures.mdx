## Summary

Microadventures makes the case that adventure does not require a sabbatical, an expensive expedition, or even a weekend. Humphreys — who has walked across continents — argues that the spirit of adventure is available on a Tuesday night within five miles of your front door. The book is a permission slip to stop waiting for the ideal moment and start experiencing small, wild, memorable things now. The practical framework is simple: sleep outside, explore a local river, walk through the night, see a sunrise from somewhere unfamiliar. Implement it by choosing one micro-adventure per month and putting it on the calendar before you can talk yourself out of it. The deeper point is about mindset: novelty, presence, and discomfort are available anywhere — and pursuing them regularly makes you more alive, more grateful, and more interesting.
