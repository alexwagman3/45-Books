## Summary

Raj Raghunathan explores why high-achieving people often undermine their own happiness by chasing status, control, and comparison. He combines behavioral research with practical habits that help shift from success-driven anxiety toward a life oriented around meaning, connection, and sustainable joy. The book identifies seven deadly happiness sins — devaluing happiness, chasing superiority, being desperate for love, being controlling, distrusting others, being overly self-critical, and ignoring the body — and offers a corrective practice for each. Read this if you are high-functioning but chronically dissatisfied. Implement it by auditing your daily decisions for whether they prioritize flourishing or just achievement, then practicing one counterintuitive habit per month: volunteering, expressing gratitude deliberately, prioritizing mastery over performance.
