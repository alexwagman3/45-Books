## Summary

Coleman reframes evangelism from event-driven outreach to a long-term disciple-making strategy modeled by Jesus. The core claim is simple but costly: Christ did not build a movement through mass messaging alone, but by investing deeply in a few who would multiply into many. The book walks through Jesus' method: selection, association, consecration, impartation, demonstration, delegation, supervision, and reproduction. Read this if you want evangelism that is sustainable, biblical, and transferable across generations. Implement it by identifying a small group to disciple personally, sharing life with them, teaching obedience not just information, and training them to disciple others. This approach may feel slower at first, but it produces durable fruit because people are formed, not merely informed.
