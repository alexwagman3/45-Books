## Summary

Tim Keller confronts suffering with theological depth and pastoral realism: pain is not rare disruption but a central feature of life in a fallen world. The book explains why suffering destabilizes identity, how different worldviews handle loss, and how the Christian story offers both honest lament and durable hope. Keller does not minimize grief; he teaches how to endure it through prayer, community, meaning, and a deeper union with God that often forms only in affliction. Read this if you want categories for sorrow that are intellectually credible and spiritually grounding. Implement it by preparing before crisis, practicing truthful prayer in hardship, receiving support from the church, and reframing trials as places where character, compassion, and trust in God can mature.
