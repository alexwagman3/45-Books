However, you should not marry without their approval. \u201cChildren, obey your parents in the Lord: for this is right\u201d (Eph. 6:1).</p> <p class="excerpt-quote">4. Declare a moratorium on media for at least one year. \u201cYe husbands, dwell with them according to knowledge, giving honour unto the wife. . . . Be ye all of one mind\u201d (1 Peter 3:7\u20138). I used to say simply, \u201cDo not get a TV set for at least one year.\u201d This was one of the most surprising things that young people heard from me. It may sound absurd. But did you know that television can be the greatest, most subtle thief of your time? It will steal away those moments that you should be devoting to your mate and, later, to your family.</p> <p class="excerpt-quote">5. First, study the Scripture, then study your wife. Dwell with her. Be totally at ease together, with full knowledge of each other. This is what marriage is all about.</p> <p class="excerpt-quote">6. Resolve negative attitudes toward each other by the end of the day or do not go to bed until you do. \u201cLet not the sun go down upon your wrath\u201d (Eph. 4:26). \u201cForgive as the Lord forgave you\u201d (Col. 3:13 NIV).</p> <p class="excerpt-quote">7. The wife hath not power of her own body, but the husband: and likewise also the husband hath not power of his own body, but the wife. Defraud ye not one the other\u201d (1 Cor. 7:3\u20135).</p> <p class="excerpt-quote">8. Have Bible study together every day.</p> <p class="excerpt-quote">9. Submission is the most important gift a wife can give her husband. A responsive and receptive wife willingly demonstrates that she surrenders her freedom for his love, adoration, protection, and provision.</p> <p class="excerpt-quote">10. Recognize that you can be the instrument through whom God will communicate His love to your partner. Pray and commit yourself to this.</p>
